Autodesk FormIt 360

×

Problem Statement


Is the interface of Autodesk FormIt 360 easy to understand and use? Are the icons intuitive and natural?

Problem Description


Autodesk Formit 360 is an architectural modeling software for architects to sketch, collaborate, analyze and share early-stage design concepts. The Autodesk team wanted a comprehensive study of the overall usability of the product for completely new users. They wanted to understand what first-time users experience while using the product, specifically in terms of discoverability of tools and usability of information palettes.

Project Details


Duration:
January 2017 - April 2017

Role:
UX Researcher, Participant Recruiter

Key Takeaways


  • Domain-specific products require that we have a basic understanding of the domain to ensure that no key issues are missed or misinterpreted.
  • It is challenging to reconcile individual differences in a subjective process like 3D representation to make a product suitable for everyone.

Proposed Solution


We recommended to Autodesk that they improve discoverability in their software by adding labels to icons and changing the layout of menu and function bars. We advised that they reconsider their icon design to make it more understandable and more consistent across the software.

Process


In order to help Autodesk FormIt 360 to improve their product, our group employed a variety of UX research methods such as:

  • Interaction map
  • User interviews
  • Comparative analysis
  • Survey design and analysis
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • Usability Test

Throughout the project, we uncovered similar usability issues with each of our methods such as lack of system feedback for certain actions, a poor mapping between icons and their meanings and inconsistencies in system behavior for similar actions.

A detailed description of each of the steps in our process can be found below.

Interaction Map

We mapped all possible interactions that a user can perform with the software and represented it in the form of an interaction map inspired by the concepts of object-oriented programming. This activity enabled us to familiarise ourselves with the software and get a better understanding of how users might interact with different UI elements within the software.

Interviews & Personas

We then proceeded to recruit potential users for interviews. We tried to recruit users representing all possible user groups and ended up interviewing 3 graduate students with varying levels of experience and backgrounds and one former architect professionals.

Based on our user interviews, we created personas which represented each user group and generated user scenarios for each of the personas.

Comparative Analysis

We compared FormIt 360 with 9 different competitor software intended for the same or similar use. We evaluated each software based on 10 criteria and assigned letter grades which marked the performance of each software in that category

This led us to discover some areas where FormIt 360 has an edge over other software in the market and also identify areas for improvement.

Survey Design and Analysis

We designed a survey to address key questions about FormIt 360's current usage, user preferences and frustrations.We grouped our survey questions into 5 sections each of which addressed one of our key questions.

We released the final survey to the FormIt 360 mailing list and received 126 responses within 2 weeks.

Heuristic Evaluation

We conducted a heuristic evaluation of FormIt 360 using the Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics. We went through every aspect of the system in multiple passes, with a subset of 3 or 4 heuristics being evaluated through each pass.

Problem identified Heuristic violated Comments
“Make unique" interaction Visibility of system status No indication of successful completion of the “Make unique" action.
Help users recognize, diagnose and recover from errors There is no way to add an unique object back to the group.
Recognition rather than recall Users need to remember which object was made unique as there is no visual difference between objects in the group and unique object.
Clarity of icons Aesthetic and minimalist design Context menu has too many icons.
Match between system and the real world Some non-standard icons do not convey the action being performed.
Recognition rather than recall Icons without labels increase cognitive load for users.
Inconsistent shortcuts Consistency and standards Some of the shortcuts are “alt+", some are single letters and some are a combination of letters.
Recognition rather than recall Too many shortcuts to remember.

Usability Test

Our next step was to perform usability tests with first-time users of FormIt 360 to validate problems previously discovered and uncover new problems. We recruited participants from the graduate cohort at the College of Architecture, University of Michigan.

From our usability test, we generated the findings listed below

  • Users had trouble discovering actions which were not explicitly provided in the menu like “apply materials" and “extrude"
  • Users preferred to use menu bar instead of icons because the icons without labels were hard to interpret.
