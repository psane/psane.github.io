TheRide Transit App

Problem Statement

How to make public transit information more reliable and accessible to commuters in Ann Arbor?

Problem Description

TheRide public transit is managed by the Ann Arbor Transportation Authority (AATA). Access to information about bus schedules and routes is available on the AATA website, but many regular commuters don't know about these services. According to a demographic survey conducted in October 2015, only 24% of commuters use the website to access transit information.

I wanted to create a solution which would help commuters access this information easily and plan their daily commute more efficiently.

Project Details

Duration:
September 2016 - December 2016

Role:
UX Designer

Key Takeaways

  • Constant user feedback is important if we hope to solve an actual real-world problem.
  • When designing for a very broad audience with different levels of technical expertise and technology access, certain adjustments have to be made to the design to make it suitable for all.

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is a smartphone app which provides real-time tracking of TheRide buses and a trip planner which can suggest possible routes for a selected destination. Users can add routes to a watch list and get push notifications when the bus is about to arrive at their stop.

Process

The goal of the project was to identify a community issue, develop a solution, test the solution with users, and then develop a prototype. I chose transit information as my problem space due to my own negative experiences of commuting within Ann Arbor.

I began the project by conducting informal interviews with peers to understand how they commute within Ann Arbor and get a sense of the user needs in this domain. I then performed a competitor analysis to understand other apps and services which aim to solve this probelm.

Through brainstorming sketches and storyboarding, I refined my design and conducted 5-minute usability tests with participants using paper prototypes. The digital prototype was created using Photoshop and Invision.

User Interviews

I interviewed several potential users to understand how they communte in Ann Arbor and how they use the existing services for planning their commutes. Through the interviews, I discovered that not many people are aware of the services such as live tracking and trip planning on the transit authority's website. Following are some user quotes from the interviews which provided the motivations for my design.

Competitive Analysis

I compared features of the transit authority's website with commonly used services and apps mentioned during user interviews. Google Maps was the most frequently mentioned tool. There were also some users who used other apps like Moovit and Transit.

Sketching & Storyboarding

I brainstormed and sketched multiple solutions which consisted of both technical and non-technical solutions. I then created scenarios in which a user might use each of these solutions and represented them through storyboarding. This helped me to focus on user needs and streamline my design to address issues the most important issues highlighted during user interviews.

Paper Prototyping

I chose to create a paper prototype for a mobile application with three basic functions - live tracking, in-app chat and helpline feature. I included the helpline feature keeping in mind users who are not very tech-savvy and would have trouble using the live tracking feature. I conducted quick usability tests with two peers to observe how they interact with the system.

Visual Design

Based on the usability tests, I made adjusments to the design such as removing the chat feature and adding trip planning. I then moved on to creating a hi-fidelity digital prototype using Photoshop and InVision. I kept the design graphically simple to prevent users from being distracted and encourage them to focus only on the features.
