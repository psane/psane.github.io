Problem Statement


How can we expand the functionality of OERCommons Hub Dashboard to meet the growing data needs of Network Hub admins?

Problem Description


OERCommons, launched by ISKME in 2007, is a freely accessible online library of over 50,000 open educational resources (OER) available for anyone to use. Network Hubs on OERCommons are resource centers managed by organizations or initiatives that bring educators together to create, collaborate and share OER.

Data reporting needs of Network Hub admins were not being met by existing reporting tools and ISKME had received numerous requests for additional data about activity on the Network Hubs.

Project Details


Duration:
May 2017 - August 2017

Role:
UX Researcher, UX Designer

Key Takeaways


  • Collaborating and communicating with a distributed team presents unique challenges.
  • Learning to tackle unforeseen challenges like last minute cancellations, incorrect participant selection, is necessary for a project's success.

Proposed Solution


Based on my user research, I provided ISKME with the following recommendations.

  • Allow users to remove data that they don’t need to track.
  • Provide users with options to view different types of data on the same screen.
  • Introduce pageviews to determine popular content and activity levels.
  • Provide multiple options for downloading reports.
  • Use filters to filter out internal traffic and increase accuracy.

I also created wireframes to demonstrate what some of these recommendations might look like on the dashboard.

Process


I began the project by conducting some background research into ISKME and the OERCommons platform. Based on this research and the project scope, I crafted a research plan which outlined the methods to be employed and the expected outcomes of the project.

User Interviews

Through video conferencing software, I interviewed six participants from different organizations with different roles within the organizations. I asked participants to walk me through their tasks on the dashboard and discuss any problems they regularly face. The goal of this user research was to understand how users are using the dashboard, what problems they are facing and what their expectations were for a new Hub Dashboard.

Wireframing

During the interviews, I discovered a number of problems faced by the users while using the Hub Dashboard. Users were unsure of the validity of the data being tracked and would get confused by the language used on the dashboard. Users also felt that their data needs we not being fully met by the dashboard and wished to see additional types of data and data representations on the dashboard.

To address these problems, I created wireframes to test specific design features like data comparison between groups. I completely changed the overview page to display key “at-a-glance” data for all activity and snippets of important data from other sections. I also introduced new data widgets to show most popular content and most active members and groups.

Usability Tests

I conducted semi-formal usability tests with four active users of the OER Commons website. Rather than providing users with specific tasks on the wireframes, I asked open-ended questions about various parts of the interface and asked them to evaluate the wireframes based on their previous experiences with similar applications. Through the user testing of the digital prototypes, I was able to understand user response to design changes in the Hub dashboard. It also uncovered issues with the digital prototypes and additional user needs that had not been voiced before.

To better understand user feedback from the usability tests, I created this affinity wall to recognize what design decisions were well received, and what changes to implement in the next iteration of the wireframes.

Impact of the Project


I was able to provide ISKME with a better understanding of their users’ behaviors, needs, and motivations. The new design of the Hub dashboard provided a good starting point for implementing changes which improve the user experience for admin users. However, due to the size of the project, development of new features can be a lengthy and complicated process.
