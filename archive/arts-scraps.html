×

Problem Statement


How can the exisiting website be leveraged to boost sales and increase participation in programs.

Problem Description


Arts and Scraps is a Detroit-based non-profit organization which provides hands-on learning and creative experiences to children living in low income areas through programs emphasizing on STEM (science, technology, engineering, math) education. They wanted to redesign their website to increase sales in their online and local retail store. They were looking for a modern and aesthetic design which brings focus to their programs and products.

Project Details


Duration:
February 2017 - March 2017

Role:
Information Architect, UI Designer

Key Takeaways


  • For a short term project, it is important to scope out the project carefully to make sure the project is completed on time.
  • Due to a lack of resources, non-profit organizations may be less willing to dedicate resources towards UX projects. It is important to communicate to them the value UX adds to their organization.

Proposed Solution


I created high-fidelity mockups of the website interface in Axure. I redesigned some pages to make the information on the pages easier to access and understand, and created the visual design for pages which were not part of the website originally.

Process


I began by meeting with the Executive and Associate Directors of Arts & Scraps to learn more about the organization and gauge their expectations from the project. Since it was a week-long project, we spent time making sure the scope was clearly defined and achievable in the short timespan.

Changing Site structure

A cursory exploration of the website revealed that the site has a lot of broken links and redundant pages which needed to be cleaned up. I began by analyzing the site structure to understand how best to restructure the website.

Working together with the management team, we removed redundant pages, and reorganized different wesbite sections to make it easier to find information on the website.

UI Mockups

The goal was to create an aesthetic and logically simple web interface. I decided to use the Arts & Scraps logo to derive a color scheme for the website to make the look more cohesive. The typeface used in the website, Josefin Slab, is very readable and has a very modern feel.

Impact of the Project


After this website redesign, Arts and Scraps will have better outreach with the community and have a more meaningful engagement with their target audience. The redesigned e-commerce platform will bring their products to better focus and increase their profits.
